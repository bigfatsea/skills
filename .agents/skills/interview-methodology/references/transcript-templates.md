# Transcript Organization — Templates & SOP

Reference for SKILL.md Mode 2. Read this when organizing and structuring interview transcripts.

---

## E.1 AI Transcription Error Correction — 3-Round SOP

**Core principle:** Never use an AI-generated transcript without human verification. Mandarin ASR Word Error Rate is typically 5–15%; dialect, elderly speakers, or specialized vocabulary can reach 30%+.

> **Critical:** The organizer must work with the original audio open — not just the transcript text. Reading a transcript reveals words; listening to the audio reveals tone, pacing, emotional intensity, paralinguistic signals, and the pauses that the transcript cannot capture. Audio is the primary source; the transcript is a derivative.

### Common AI Transcription Error Types (Chinese)

- **Homophone substitutions:** 的/得/地, 在/再, 做/作, 他/她/它
- **Dialect / accent errors:** Characters that sound similar in the narrator's regional variety
- **High-error categories:** Numbers, proper nouns (names, place names), dates, specialized terminology
- **Multi-speaker confusion:** Multiple speakers' turns mixed or misattributed
- **Paralinguistic markers:** Laughter, sighs, pauses transcribed as garbled text
- **Code-switching:** English words in Chinese speech typically have lower recognition accuracy

### The 3-Round Correction Protocol

| Round | Action | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| **Round 1: Skim** | Read for overall comprehension; mark every unreadable or implausible passage as `[?]` | Transcript text only |
| **Round 2: Audio Alignment** | For every `[?]` passage, listen to the original audio and correct or mark `[uncertain]` | Audio player (Audacity / QuickTime / variable-speed player) |
| **Round 3: Context Verification** | Check all proper nouns, dates, and specialist terms for internal consistency across the full transcript | Full-text search |

**After correction, mark passages as:**
- `[verified]` — confirmed against original audio
- `[uncertain]` — audio unclear; best approximation, note ambiguity
- `[inaudible]` — cannot be recovered even from audio

### E.2 Organizing Principles — Five Goals

Every organized transcript must achieve:

1. **Faithful to original content** — do not invent or remove core meaning
2. **Cover all key points** — do not omit significant information
3. **Clear narrative through-line** — reorganize by time or theme as appropriate
4. **Accurate and concise language** — remove filler phrases, repetitions, and tangents
5. **Capture subtext** — record nonverbal signals, emotional cues, contextual associations

### Common Organizer Errors to Avoid

- ❌ Beautifying the narrator's language (replacing colloquial with formal)
- ❌ Removing content the narrator might find embarrassing
- ❌ Reconciling contradictions into a single coherent version
- ❌ Omitting markers for pauses, silences, and emotional moments
- ❌ Embedding the organizer's own interpretations inside the notes body

## E.3 Five Interview Type Templates

### E.3.1 Life Experience (Primary Template)

```markdown
# [Narrator name / pseudonym] — Interview Notes · v[n]
> Organization date: [date]
> Organized by: [name]
> Recording length: [X] minutes
> Original recording: [file link or path]

## 1. Core Information
- Narrator: [name, pseudonym, birth year]
- Summary of main experiences: [2–3 sentences]
- Context at time of recording: [e.g., "age 70, recovering from illness"]

## 2. Timeline (Main Axis)

### Early Years (0–18)
- [YYYY] [Event]
- [YYYY] [Event]
- Key turning point: [...]

### Middle Years (19–40)
- [YYYY] [Event]
- ...

### Recent Years (40–present)
- [YYYY] [Event]
- ...

## 3. Thematic Clusters

### Theme 1: [e.g., "Influence of mother"]
- Direct quote: "[original text]"
- Context of occurrence: [description]
- Emotional tone: [moved / angry / nostalgic / ...]

### Theme 2: [e.g., "Career choice"]
- ...

### Theme 3: [e.g., "The end of a relationship"]
- ...

## 4. Key Relationship Map

| Person | Relationship | Key event | Current status |
|---|---|---|---|
| | | | |

## 5. Subtext / Hidden Signals

- [Timestamp] [Context] — word before pause: "[...]", may indicate [inference]
- [Timestamp] Topic avoided: [topic] — possible reason: [inference]
- [Timestamp] Emotional intensity shift: [description]
- [Timestamp] Contradiction (same event told differently): [description]
- [Timestamp] Body language noted: [description]

## 6. Unresolved Questions / Follow-Up Targets

- [ ] Question 1
- [ ] Question 2

## 7. Organizer Notes
- Overall impression
- Organizational challenges
- Recommendations for next steps
```

## E.4 Subtext Capture Table

Complete this table as a separate section in the organized notes, not embedded in the main narrative.

| Signal type | What to note | Marker format |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-pause word** | The word(s) spoken immediately before a notable pause | `[pause before] "..."` — possible significance |
| **Avoided topic** | A topic the narrator steered away from | `[avoided] [topic] — possible reason` |
| **Recurring theme word** | A word or phrase that reappears across the session | `[recurring] [word] × N occurrences — possible deep concern` |
| **Emotional intensity shift** | Sudden animation or sudden flattening | `[sudden animation] / [sudden calm] [timestamp]` |
| **Contradiction** | Same event or person described differently at different moments | `[contradiction] [timestamp]: "[Version A]" vs "[Version B]"` |
| **Body language** | Eye aversion, clenched fists, deep breath, postural change | `[body: averted gaze / clenched hands / deep breath] [timestamp]` |

**Note:** AI transcription tools do not record body language. These must come from the interviewer's in-session notes or immediate post-session recall.
